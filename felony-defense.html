Why this is different

A felony is not a traffic ticket with higher stakes. It is a different kind of fight.

A conviction can take your liberty, your right to vote and to carry a firearm, your professional licenses, your standing in your community, and your ability to earn a living long after any sentence ends. Prosecutors know this. They build felony cases methodically, with investigators and resources, often for months before you ever see the inside of a courtroom.

Meeting that requires more than a defense. It requires someone who has stood in these courtrooms for decades, who knows how the local district attorneys think, how the judges rule, and where the State's case is weakest — and who is willing to make the prosecution prove every element of every charge.
